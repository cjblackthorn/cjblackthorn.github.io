<p><strong>C. J. Blackthorn</strong> is a writer, survivor, technologist, and disability advocate.</p>

<p>Her work explores the systems people are forced to survive: healthcare, poverty, technology, relationships, trauma, accessibility, disability, and power.</p>

<p>She writes with the belief that people are not broken because they struggle. Often, they are struggling because the systems around them were never built to hold them.</p>

<p><strong>Blackthorn Field Notes</strong> was created as a home for essays on disability, advocacy, systems failure, technology, relationships, survival, and the work of staying human in a world that often asks too much and gives too little back.</p>

<p>Her writing lives at the intersection of the personal and the structural. Some essays are rooted in lived experience. Some are rooted in analysis. Most are both. She writes about what it means to survive broken systems, navigate access barriers, examine power honestly, and keep hold of your humanity anyway.</p>

<p>Before launching Blackthorn Field Notes, C. J. Blackthorn built a deep background in technology and cybersecurity, bringing a systems-minded lens to everything she does. That perspective carries through her work: she does not just ask what hurts, but what system made the harm possible, who benefits from it, and what must change.</p>

<p>At its core, this publication exists for people who have had to survive more than they should have, for people who want clearer language for the realities they live inside, and for people who still believe that truth, accountability, and compassion matter.</p>

<p>Blackthorn Field Notes is not just a collection of posts. It is a record of survival, observation, and refusal. A place to name what is happening, to question what others accept too easily, and to keep reaching for something more human.</p>
